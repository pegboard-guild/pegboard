# The Forum — Civic Accountability Layer

## Core Concept

Every government action in the Graph — every vote, budget allocation, contract award — is a node that citizens can react to.

## Features

### Citizen Reactions
- **👍 👎 on any government action** — votes, budget items, contracts
- Aggregated counts visible on every node ("4,200 citizens opposed this")
- Reactions tied to verified residents (future: address-based verification per jurisdiction)

### Anchored Comments
- Every comment is attached to a specific data node — a vote, a contract, a budget line
- No free-floating opinions. You comment ON something, with the data right there.
- "Opinions come with receipts"

### Representative Scorecards
- Citizen approval rating per official, derived from reactions to their votes
- Side-by-side: how they voted vs how their constituents reacted
- Campaign donor overlay: who funded them vs how citizens feel about their votes

### Budget Comparison
- When a spending item is approved, show what else that money could fund
- "$500M stadium = 12 new schools or 200 miles of road repair"
- Sourced from the same budget data, not editorialized

### Follow the Money → React
- See a contract awarded to a donor → react
- See a vote that benefits a donor → react  
- The graph makes connections visible. The forum lets citizens respond.

## What This Is NOT
- Not a social network (no profiles, no followers, no feed algorithm)
- Not a petition platform (reactions inform, they don't compel)
- Not editorialized (the Graph is pure data; the Forum is citizen voice; the separation is sacred)

## Why This Matters
Every existing civic engagement tool is either:
- **Data without voice** (OpenSecrets, USAspending) — you can look, but not respond
- **Voice without data** (Twitter, NextDoor, city council public comment) — you can yell, but nothing is anchored to fact

Pegboard is both. Data that citizens can see AND respond to, with every opinion attached to the evidence.
