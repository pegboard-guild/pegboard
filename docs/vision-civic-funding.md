# Civic Funding — Community-Pooled Action

## Core Concept

Citizens can pool money toward societal improvements, anchored to specific government data nodes. See the problem in the data → react → fund the solution.

## Models

### 1. Civic Bounties
- Attached to a specific issue surfaced by the graph (a neglected budget item, a failed bill, a broken infrastructure project)
- Citizens pledge money toward a defined outcome
- Outcome verified against graph data when possible (e.g., "road repaired" confirmed by city contract data)
- Funds held in escrow, released on completion or refunded

### 2. Advocacy Pools
- Attached to a bill, policy area, or pattern surfaced by the graph
- Citizens pool funds directed to vetted nonprofits, advocacy groups, or legal funds working on that issue
- Transparent: every dollar tracked, every recipient visible
- "84% of residents opposed this vote. $47K pooled for advocacy."

### 3. Community Infrastructure Fund
- Data shows government underinvestment in a specific area
- Citizens fund the gap directly — park cleanup, intersection safety study, youth program
- Graph data provides the evidence ("$0 allocated to District 4 parks in FY2025")

## Principles

- **Data-anchored:** Every funding pool is tied to a specific graph node (bill, budget line, contract, vote)
- **Transparent:** All contributions and disbursements visible on the platform
- **Non-partisan:** Fund outcomes, not candidates. This is NOT campaign finance.
- **Auditable:** Full provenance, same as the graph data itself

## Legal Considerations (Research Needed)
- Money transmission licensing (state-by-state)
- 501(c)(3) vs 501(c)(4) fiscal sponsor model
- Campaign finance law — must NOT fund candidates or campaigns
- Lobbying registration thresholds
- Securities law if structured as investment
- **Likely approach:** Partner with existing fiscal sponsor (e.g., Open Collective Foundation) rather than handle money directly

## Why This Matters

Current civic engagement: see problem → complain on Twitter → nothing happens.

Pegboard civic funding: see problem (with data) → react (with receipts) → fund the fix (with accountability).

The graph provides evidence. The forum provides voice. The funding provides action. Complete civic loop.
