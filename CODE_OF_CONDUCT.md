# Code of Conduct

## The Short Version

Be decent. Assume good faith. Focus on the work.

This project exists to make government data accessible to citizens. That mission is bigger than any individual contributor, maintainer, or disagreement.

## Standards

**Do:**
- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions
- Accept feedback gracefully and give it kindly
- Focus on what's best for the project and its users
- Credit others' work

**Don't:**
- Harass, insult, or demean other contributors
- Make it personal — critique code, not people
- Use the project or its spaces for partisan political advocacy (the tool is nonpartisan by design)
- Spam, troll, or derail discussions

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — issues, PRs, discussions, and any official channels.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will remove comments, close issues, or ban contributors who violate this code. If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers.

## Note on Political Neutrality

Pegboard surfaces government data without editorial bias. Contributors may have strong political opinions — that's fine, and expected from people who care about civic life. But the codebase, documentation, and project spaces must remain nonpartisan. The tool serves all citizens regardless of political affiliation.
